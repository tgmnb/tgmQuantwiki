## 摘要

Human Motion Segmentation (HMS), which aims to partition a video into non-overlapping segments corresponding to different human motions, has recently attracted increasing research attention. Existing HMS approaches are predominantly based on subspace clustering, which are grounded on the assumption that the distribution of high-dimensional temporal features well aligns with a Union-of-Subspaces (UoS).
