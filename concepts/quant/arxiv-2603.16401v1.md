## 摘要

Constrained multi-objective optimization problems (CMOPs) are of great significance in the context of practical applications, ranging from scientific to engineering domains. Most existing constrained multi-objective evolutionary algorithms (CMOEAs) usually employ fixed operators all the time, which exhibit poor versatility in handling various CMOPs. Therefore, some recent studies have focused on adaptively selecting the best operators for the current population states during the search process.
