# Optimal Insurance Menu Design under the Expected-Value Premium Principle

**ArXiv ID:** [2604.15881v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15881v1)

## Abstract

This paper studies optimal insurance design under asymmetric information in a Stackelberg framework, where a monopolistic insurer faces uncertainty about both the insured's risk attitude, captured by a risk-aversion parameter, and the insured's risk type, characterized by the loss distribution. In particular, when the risk type is unobservable, we allow the risk-aversion parameter to depend on the risk type. We construct a menu of contracts that maximizes the mean-variance utilities of both part
