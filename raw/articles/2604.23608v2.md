## Abstract

Financial markets are often modelled as if time were unique and continuous across assets and markets. Financial markets are however asynchronous, order flow is event-driven, and waiting times between events are often random. Many of the most influential formulations of financial market models presuppose a unique global calendar time and advocate for this or that preferred single latent continuous-time price system.
