## Abstract

Human demonstrations provide strong priors for robot manipulation, yet it is non-trivial to transfer them to execute on real robots due to the kinematic gap. In dexterous manipulation, it remains challenging to track long-horizon, contact-rich sequences even in simulators: a reference-tracking policy must keep objects on their target trajectories while preserving demonstrated joint motion and contact timing.
