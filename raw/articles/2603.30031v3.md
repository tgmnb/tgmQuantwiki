## Abstract

Autonomous tool-using agents in networked environments must decide which information source to query and when to stop querying and act. Without principled bounds on information-acquisition costs, unconstrained agents exhibit systematic failure modes: excessive tool use under congestion, prolonged deliberation under time decay, and brittle behavior under ambiguous evidence.
