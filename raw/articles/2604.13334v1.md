## Abstract

We investigate the impossibility of universally winning trading strategies -- those generating strict profit across all market trajectories -- through three distinct mathematical paradigms. Fundamentally, under standard admissibility constraints, the existence of such a strategy is a strict subset of strong arbitrage, which is mathematically precluded in competitive markets admitting an equivalent martingale measure.
