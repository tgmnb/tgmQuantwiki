## Abstract

We characterize optimal monetary policy when policy endogenously moves risk premia through redistribution across agents who differ in their willingness to bear risk. The analytical core is Marginal Risk Capacity, the covariance of monetary policy exposures with marginal propensities to take risk. This sufficient statistic governs this channel as MPCs govern the consumption channel.
