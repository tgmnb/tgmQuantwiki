# Anomalous Hall Conductivity as an Effective Means of Tracking the Floquet Weyl Nodes in Quasi-One-Dimensional $β$-Bi$_4$I$_4$

## Abstract

While Floquet engineering offers a powerful paradigm for manipulating topological phases, particularly Floquet Weyl semimetals, establishing an experimentally feasible strategy for tracking the dynamic evolution of such states remains a significant challenge. Here, we propose that the anomalous Hall effect (AHE), as a sensitive, all-electrical probe, can be used to track Floquet Weyl nodes.
