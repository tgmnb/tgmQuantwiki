## Abstract

We derive equations of motion for capital deepening in a competitive economy directly from accounting identities, without assuming a production function. A profit imperative $η^* \equiv (w/κ+ 1/τ)/(1-f_p)$ sets the minimum viable capital productivity, where $η= Y/K$ [yr$^{-1}$] is capital productivity, $κ= K/L$ is capital per worker, $w$ is the wage rate, $τ$ is the capital lifetime, and $f_p$ is the production tax share.
