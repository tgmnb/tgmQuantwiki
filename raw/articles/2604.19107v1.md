## Abstract

We identify a robust structural signature of stock markets during exogenous shock events by analyzing collective return dynamics across G5 countries. Using Random Matrix Theory, we introduce the complexity gap, defined as the difference between the normalized largest eigenvalue and the average pairwise correlation, to quantify changes in market structure.
