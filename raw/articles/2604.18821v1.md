## Abstract

Institutional allocators often evaluate structured strategies on the basis of marketed backtests -- hypothetical track records constructed by applying a strategy's rules to historical data prior to any live trading, also referred to as pro-forma performance. It is unclear how much of that signal survives once the strategy is actually traded.
