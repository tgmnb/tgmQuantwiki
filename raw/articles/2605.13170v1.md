## Abstract

Multi-agent systems rely on communication for information sharing and action coordination, which exposes a vulnerability to attacks. We investigate single-victim communication perturbation attacks against Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning-trained systems and propose methods that use gradient information from the Jacobian to identify which messages, agent, and timesteps are most susceptible to attack and have the greatest impact on the system.
