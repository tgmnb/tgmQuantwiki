# How the interpolation of life tables affects the decomposition of life insurance surplus

**ArXiv ID:** [2606.04715](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04715)
**PDF:** [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.04715](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.04715)
**Published:** 2026-06-04
**Categories:** Statistical Finance (q-fin.ST)
**Authors:** Mintodê Nicodème Atchadé, Marcus C. Christiansen, Friedrich Hubalek, Gero Junike

## Abstract

The surplus of a life insurance policy depends on both systematic changes in mortality risk and financial changes. We propose to decompose the surplus by the axiomatically justified IASU decomposition, which is a continuous time version of the Shapley value. However, life tables are not updated continuously, but rather, only once per year.
