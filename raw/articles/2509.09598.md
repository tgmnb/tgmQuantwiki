# Ancestral origins of environmental (in)attention

**ArXiv ID:** [2509.09598](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.09598)
**PDF:** [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.09598](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.09598)
**Published:** 2026-06-04
**Categories:** General Economics (econ.GN)
**Authors:** César Barilla, Palaash Bhargava

## Abstract

How does the climatic experience of past generations affect today&#39;s attitudes towards environmental issues? Using empirical evidence spanning multiple contemporary surveys and ethnic group level cultural records, we show that the intensity of ancestral climate anomalies has a persistent effect on the perceived stakes of environmental considerations in decision-making.
