## Abstract

The rise of text-to-image (T2I) models has increasingly raised concerns regarding the generation of risky content, such as sexual, violent, and copyright-protected images, highlighting the need for effective safeguards within the models themselves. Although existing methods have been proposed to eliminate risky concepts from T2I models, they are primarily developed for earlier U-Net architectures, leaving the state-of-the-art Diffusion-Transformer-based T2I models inadequately protected.
