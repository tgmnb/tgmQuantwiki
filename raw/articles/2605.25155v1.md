## Abstract

Isotopically non-stationary metabolic flux analysis (INST $^{13}$C-MFA) provides unique insights into cellular physiology but is typically limited by low throughput and high experimental costs. Here, we present a miniaturized and automated workflow that integrates transient isotope labeling experiments with advanced computational modeling to enable parallel INST $^{13}$C-MFA at microliter scale.
