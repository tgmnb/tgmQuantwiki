## Abstract

Put-call parity is a terminal-payoff identity, but its enforcement is capital-using. I study the carry gap, the annualized wedge between option-implied and OIS discount factors, in SPX and RUT index options. Quoted parity is tightly compressed, while the synthetic-traded forward channel leaves a systematic wedge. I interpret this wedge as an implementation premium under finite arbitrage capital.
