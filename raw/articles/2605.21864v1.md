## Abstract

Understanding how typhoons respond to localized perturbations in their environmental fields is fundamental to assessing the limits of predictability and exploring the potential for track or intensity intervention. This study develops a dedicated simulation methodology testbed for typhoon sensitivity analysis by integrating the Pangu weather model, a high-precision AI forecasting system, with Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) closed-loop techniques.
