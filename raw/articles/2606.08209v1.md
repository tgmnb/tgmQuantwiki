# Markets Are Not Random, They Are Hard to Predict

**ArXiv ID:** [2606.08209v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08209v1)

## Abstract

Financial returns are often called ``random,'' but the word conflates ontic chance, epistemic ignorance, strategic feedback, and model instability. This essay argues that financial markets are not random in the ontic sense in which a quantum measurement is random. They are causal economic systems whose future is hard to predict because relevant causes are hidden, costly to observe, strategically used, capacity constrained, and sometimes governed by a changing law.
