## Abstract

Collateralized prediction markets are contingent-claim markets in which economic uncertainty can disappear before winning claims become redeemable. This paper studies the pricing effect of that delay. When collateral remains locked until oracle settlement, a near-certain dollar is a delayed dollar, so prices embed a maturity-dependent settlement discount in addition to beliefs about outcomes.
