## Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have emerged as a promising foundation for universal multimodal embeddings. Recent studies have shown that reasoning-driven generative multimodal embeddings can outperform discriminative embeddings on several embedding tasks. However, Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning tends to generate redundant thinking steps and introduce semantic ambiguity in the summarized answers in broader retrieval scenarios.
